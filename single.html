Day 1:

Say Goodbye To My Baby

    • Ut ma quae. Vidustias con est.

    I had just quit a good job that I had moved to New York City for, mostly out of my tendency to be unsatisfied with everything. Something was nagging at me, something that was not work related. I spent a lonely night drinking whiskey at my local bar one night and knew I had to make a big decision in my life. Did I want to spend the rest of my life in New York City or should I turn my tail and run towards the warm, inviting west coast?

    I set bold goals for my career, I felt New York City would fulfill these ambitions, and in a way it had. But after I quit my job and the smoke cleared, I soon discovered I was not paying attention to the other part of my life, the time outside of work where I should have been cultivating friendships, relationships, spending time with family. My career ate up all of my time, much of this was self-imposed from this workaholic state of mind I had imposed on myself since high school.

    I needed to try and find balance in my life. My first decision was to move back to the west coast, to the Bay Area, where I'd be closer to my friends and family I felt I left behind three years ago. I also decided to take a break from work for a month and take a road trip. I bought an old station wagon for $1600, got rid of most of my belongings and piled the rest into the back of the station wagon. I hoped that when I got to the other side a month later, I would know exactly how I would want to live the rest of my life, and figure out a way to find the balance I was looking for.
